What Affects the Final Price?
All spray coating projects vary in final cost based on a number of factors. Here's what drives price up or down:

Preparation Required
The more extensive the surface preparation needed — rust treatment, repairs, multiple sanding stages, biocidal treatment — the more labour is involved before a single coat of paint is applied. We never skip preparation to hit a lower price.

Substrate Type
PVDF, Plastisol, stainless steel, galvanised steel, anodised aluminium, laminate, and melamine all require specialist primer systems that cost more than standard substrates. The product cost is reflected in the project price.

Access Requirements
Working at height on scaffolding, MEWP, or rope access adds cost — but it is never optional where required by the Work at Height Regulations 2005. If access equipment is required, we will always specify it correctly and include it in your quotation upfront.

Coating System Specified
Single-pack, two-pack, elastomeric, intumescent, and specialist silicone systems each carry different material costs. We always specify the correct system — not the cheapest one.

Project Scale & Location
Larger projects and projects requiring longer travel or overnight stays will be reflected in the overall project cost. For all projects, we provide a fixed-price written quotation following the free site survey — no hidden extras.
