What Is Conservatory Spraying?
Conservatory spraying is a professional on-site coating process that applies specialist, chemically-bonding UPVC and aluminium paints to every element of your conservatory's frame structure — including roof bars, ridge, hip bars, guttering, side frames, windows, and doors — completely transforming its colour and restoring its appearance.

Unlike standard paint, the specialist coatings we use are specifically formulated to bond directly to UPVC, powder-coated aluminium, and GRP surfaces, creating a long-lasting, weather-resistant finish that won't peel, crack, or bubble.

Our Conservatory Spraying Process
1. Free Site Survey & Consultation
We visit your property to assess the conservatory type, frame material, condition, and surface contamination levels. We discuss colour options, finish levels, and confirm the most suitable coating system for your project.

2. Deep Clean & Degreasing
Conservatory roof beams and guttering accumulate significantly more dirt and debris than window frames, requiring extra thorough cleaning. All surfaces are washed down with soap and water, followed by treatment with Kolorbond WindowPrep to remove all grease, oxidisation, and biological deposits.

3. Abrading & Keying
All UPVC and aluminium surfaces — including roof bars, ridge bars, hip bars, frames, guttering, and door profiles — are abraded using Mirka Mirlon red scotch pads to create a consistent mechanical key.

4. Full Masking & Protection
All glazing panels (polycarbonate or glass), rubber seals, brickwork, external walls, and surrounding paving are fully protected using static masking film and professional masking tape. This is the most time-consuming stage — approximately 80% of the project time is spent on preparation and masking, which is what ensures a factory-quality finish.

5. Dust Coat (Test Coat)
A very light initial 'dust coat' is applied across all surfaces. This critical step reveals any remaining contaminants — if the paint reacts adversely anywhere, the surface is re-prepared before proceeding.

6. Full Coat Application
A minimum of two further full coats are applied using professional HVLP spray equipment, achieving an even, consistent colour and finish across every frame, bar, and profile.

7. Protective Clear Coat
A UV-resistant protective mist coat is applied over the entire surface for additional durability and finish depth.

8. De-mask, Inspect & Sign Off
All masking is carefully removed. We carry out a thorough quality inspection with you on completion, checking every frame, roof bar, and joint for consistency, coverage, and finish quality.
