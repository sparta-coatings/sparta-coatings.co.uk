Our Fascia & Soffit Spraying Process
1. Free Survey & Colour Consultation
We assess the condition, material, and extent of your fascia and soffit boards — identifying any biological growth, delamination, or damage requiring repair before coating.

2. Biocidal Treatment
A fungicidal wash is applied to all surfaces and left to dwell, killing all biological growth before preparation begins.

3. Cleaning & Degreasing
All boards are cleaned with soap and water, then treated with Kolorbond WindowPrep or Vinylkleen to remove all grease and contaminants. Timber fascias are cleaned and spot-primed as required.

4. Abrading & Keying
UPVC surfaces are lightly abraded with Mirka red scotch-brite pads. Timber surfaces are sanded to bare wood or a sound existing coating using 80–120 grit, depending on condition.

5. Masking & Protection
Roof tiles, brickwork, guttering, soffits (or fascias), windows, and surrounding areas are all fully masked using professional tape and static masking film to ensure a clean, straight spray line.

6. Priming (Where Required)
Timber fascias and soffits are primed with Zinsser BullsEye 1-2-3 to seal and stabilise the surface before the topcoat. UPVC is coated directly with Kolorbond following preparation.

7. Topcoat Application
A minimum of two full coats are applied using professional HVLP spray equipment, achieving full, even coverage across all fascia profiles, soffit panels, bargeboards, and trims. Each coat is inspected before the next.

8. De-mask, Inspect & Handover
All masking is carefully removed, edges are checked for accuracy, and a full quality inspection is completed with you before we leave.
