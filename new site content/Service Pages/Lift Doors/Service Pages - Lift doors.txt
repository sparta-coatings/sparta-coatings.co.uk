What Is Lift Door Spraying?
Lift door spraying is the professional on-site application of specialist coating systems to the interior and exterior faces of lift doors, surrounds, architraves, and associated metalwork — restoring their appearance, aligning them with your brand colours, and significantly extending their service life without the enormous cost of replacement.

Using HVLP spray equipment and professional two-pack coating systems, our trained applicators deliver a smooth, hardwearing finish on all lift door surfaces — including notoriously difficult stainless steel — with no removal and minimal disruption to building operations.

Powder-coated steel lift doors

Painted mild steel lift doors

Aluminium lift doors and surrounds

Lift surrounds, architraves, and threshold strips

Internal and external lift door faces

Passenger lifts, goods lifts, and platform lifts

The Paints & Products We Use
Lift doors demand coatings that can withstand extremely high levels of daily contact, abrasion, and cleaning — and stainless steel in particular requires a specialist approach to ensure adhesion. We use only professional, high-durability coating systems designed for this environment.

Cleaning & Preparation
Isopropyl Alcohol (IPA) Wipe-Down — All lift door surfaces are thoroughly degreased with IPA to remove finger grease, cleaning product residue, oils, and atmospheric contamination before preparation begins. This is especially critical on stainless steel, where even trace contamination will cause adhesion failure

400-grit Mirka Abranet / Scotch-Brite Pads — All surfaces are mechanically abraded to create a consistent key. On stainless steel, abrading with Mirka pads cuts through the passive oxide layer that naturally forms on the surface, allowing the etch primer to bond to the bare metal beneath

Primers
2K Etch Primer (for Stainless Steel & Aluminium) — The non-negotiable foundation for any coating system applied to stainless steel or aluminium lift doors. The two-component acid-catalysed formula chemically etches into the passive oxide layer, creating an extremely strong bond that a conventional primer cannot achieve on these surfaces

Rust-Oleum 3302 CombiPrimer Adhesion Primer — For powder-coated and galvanised steel lift doors, this fast-drying, universal adhesion primer provides a chemically bonded base layer that bridges the gap between the existing surface and the new topcoat system

Zinsser BullsEye 1-2-3 — For previously painted or mixed-substrate lift surrounds and architraves, providing stain blocking and excellent topcoat adhesion across all surface types

Topcoats
2K Two-Pack Acrylic / Polyurethane Topcoat — The professional standard for lift door spraying. Two-pack catalysed systems cure chemically to an extremely hard, scratch-resistant, cleanable film — far more durable than single-pack products and specifically suited to the high-wear, high-touch environment of a commercial lift. Available in all RAL and BS colours, full range of gloss levels, and metallic finishes

PPG Selemix 2K Direct Gloss / Satin — A professional automotive-grade two-component acrylic topcoat providing outstanding hardness, chemical resistance, and colour depth. Available in the full PPG colour range and custom mixing — ideal for lift doors requiring a premium, long-lasting finish that withstands heavy commercial use

Rust-Oleum Hard Hat (2K PU) — For lift doors in commercial and industrial settings requiring maximum durability, our two-pack polyurethane topcoat provides an extremely hardwearing, abrasion-resistant finish available in gloss, satin, and matte

Water-Based 2K Acrylic (Low Odour) — For occupied buildings where solvent odour is a concern — hotels, hospitals, and residential blocks — we offer a water-based, low-odour two-pack acrylic system that cures to a durable, cleanable finish with minimal disruption to building occupants

Metallic & Specialist Finishes
Metallic Silver / Brushed Steel Effect — Achieved using specialist metallic flake lacquers to replicate a brushed stainless or metallic appearance — ideal for modernising older painted lift doors or providing consistency across a mixed fleet

Textured / Crocodile Skin Finish — An increasingly popular premium option for boutique hotels and high-end commercial properties, providing a distinctive tactile texture and hiding surface imperfections effectively

Anti-Fingerprint Clear Coat — A clear, anti-smear topcoat applied over metallic or stainless finishes to dramatically reduce visible fingerprint marks on high-traffic lift doors

Our Lift Door Spraying Process
1. Free Survey & Colour Consultation
We assess all lift doors across the building — recording surface material, condition, extent of damage, and number of floors. We discuss colour choices, finish levels, and agree an out-of-hours working schedule that keeps the lift operational throughout.

2. Out-of-Hours Scheduling
Most lift door projects are scheduled overnight or during low-footfall periods to eliminate disruption to building users. We agree the programme of works in advance with facilities management.

3. Masking & Protection
All adjacent surfaces — walls, floor thresholds, call buttons, and door mechanisms — are fully masked with professional tape and static film to prevent any overspray.

4. Cleaning & Degreasing
All door surfaces are thoroughly wiped down with IPA to remove all grease and contamination.

5. Abrading & Keying
All surfaces are mechanically keyed with appropriate abrasive pads — cutting through the oxide layer on stainless steel and powder coat on painted doors.

6. In-Situ Repair
Dents, deep scratches, and scuffs are filled with aluminium epoxy filler and feathered flat before priming.

7. Primer Application
The appropriate etch primer or adhesion primer is applied by HVLP spray, achieving a fully bonded base layer across the entire door surface.

8. Topcoat Application
A minimum of two full coats of the specified 2K topcoat are applied, achieving a consistent, even finish across both door faces and all surrounding architrave profiles.

9. De-mask, Inspect & Handover
All masking is removed, door mechanisms are checked for free operation, and a quality inspection is completed before the building re-opens.
