What Is Steelwork Spraying?
Steelwork spraying is the professional application of anti-corrosion primer systems and protective or decorative topcoats to structural steel, architectural metalwork, and industrial steel frameworks — using airless or HVLP spray equipment to achieve a consistent, high-build, even finish across all steel sections, connections, and details.

Unlike brush or roller application, airless spray delivers coating to the full specified dry film thickness (DFT) in a single pass — ensuring complete coverage of complex steel profiles including I-beams, channels, angles, hollow sections, and connection plates, which are impossible to coat uniformly by hand.

We work on all types of structural and architectural steelwork, including:

Structural steel frames and columns (I-section, H-section, CHS, RHS)

Mezzanine floors and industrial platforms

Surface Preparation Standards
Steelwork surface preparation is defined and verified against internationally recognised standards — not left to judgment.

ISO 8501-1 / BS EN ISO 8501 — The international standard for visual assessment of surface cleanliness. Key grades used:

Sa 2½ — Near white metal blast cleaning — the standard for new structural steel in C3–C5 environments

Sa 2 — Commercial blast cleaning — for moderate environment maintenance projects

St 3 — Thorough power tool cleaning — for on-site refurbishment where blast is not practicable

High-Pressure Water Jetting (1,500–3,000 PSI) — Used to remove loose rust, failed coatings, and surface contamination before mechanical or blast preparation

Abrasive Blast Cleaning (Grit / Shot Blasting) — Used on new structural steel and heavily corroded existing steelwork to achieve Sa 2½ surface cleanliness. Creates the surface profile (typically 40–75 microns Rz) required for epoxy primer adhesion

Industrial Solvent Degreaser — Applied to all steel surfaces before blasting or power tool preparation to remove all oils, grease, and soluble salts

Our Steelwork Spraying Process
1. Free Survey, Specification & Risk Assessment
We carry out a full condition survey of all steelwork, determine the corrosivity category for the environment, agree the coating specification with the client and structural engineer where applicable, and produce a full method statement and COSHH assessment.

2. HSE-Compliant Safe Access
All steelwork operations at height are carried out in full compliance with the Work at Height Regulations 2005, using appropriate MEWP, scaffold, or rope access systems as defined in the method statement.

3. Surface Preparation
Steel is prepared to the specified ISO 8501 cleanliness standard — Sa 2½ by abrasive blast cleaning for new and heavily corroded steel, St 3 power tool cleaning for on-site refurbishment. All welds, edges, and bolt holes are ground and dressed before preparation.

4. Soluble Salt Testing (Where Specified)
On high-specification C4–C5 projects, soluble salt levels are tested using Bresle patch or similar methodology before priming, ensuring compliance with ISO 8502-9 maximum salt contamination levels.

5. Primer Application with DFT Verification
The specified primer system is applied by professional airless spray, achieving the required wet and dry film thickness across all steel sections. DFT readings are taken using an approved gauge at defined intervals throughout.

6. Intermediate Coat (Where Specified)
High-build intermediate coats are applied where the total system DFT requires an additional build layer — typically on C4–C5 systems specifying total DFTs of 200–320 microns.

7. Topcoat Application
The final colour coat is applied in a minimum of two passes, achieving full, even coverage and the specified gloss level across all steel elements. Final DFT readings are taken and recorded.

8. Intumescent Fire Coat (Where Required)
Where fire protection is specified, intumescent topcoats are applied at the thickness determined by the structural engineer's fire engineering specification — then overcoated with a compatible sealer for aesthetic and durability purposes.

9. Full Project Audit, DFT Report & Sign-Off
A complete DFT inspection report is produced, covering all surface areas. The project is signed off and all documentation — including COSHH assessments, paint batch numbers, and DFT records — is issued to the client.
