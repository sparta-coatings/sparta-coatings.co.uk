The Paints & Products We Use
Garage doors face some of the harshest conditions of any surface on a property — direct UV, rain, frost, and daily mechanical use. We use only professional, substrate-specific coating systems selected for their corrosion resistance, adhesion strength, and long-term durability.

Preparation Products
Rust-Oleum or Hammerite Rust Remover / Rust Converter — For steel and metal doors showing signs of surface rust or corrosion, a rust converter is applied to chemically neutralise rust before priming, halting further oxidisation beneath the surface

Kolorbond WindowPrep — Used on UPVC and powder-coated aluminium doors as a professional degreaser and surface preparation solution, removing all contaminants prior to coating

400-grit wet-and-dry paper / Mirka Scotch-Brite Pads — All surfaces are lightly abraded to create a mechanical key for the primer and topcoat

Primers
Etch Primer (2K Epoxy Etch Primer) — Applied to bare metal, steel, and aluminium surfaces to provide a chemically etched base layer that maximises adhesion and provides corrosion resistance before the topcoat

Rust-Oleum High Build Etch Primer — A high-build option used on steel doors with light surface scratches, minor imperfections, and indentations — fills and keys the surface in one product for a smoother topcoat result

Zinsser BullsEye 1-2-3 — Used on timber and GRP garage doors as a multi-surface stain-blocking primer, ensuring excellent topcoat adhesion across mixed substrates

Topcoats
Rust-Oleum CombiColor Original — A professional primer and topcoat in one, specifically formulated for steel, metal, and rust-affected surfaces. Available in 4,400+ tintable colours including RAL 7016 Anthracite Grey, Chartwell Green, Jet Black, and custom shades. Corrosion inhibiting, lead and chromate free, with excellent UV and weather resistance

Kolorbond Original — The UK's leading UPVC-specific coating, used on all plastic and powder-coated aluminium garage doors. Chemically bonds without a primer on UPVC and PVCu, available in 5,000+ colours with a 10-year adhesion guarantee

Kolorbond K2 (2K Polyurethane) — Our premium, two-pack catalysed system for clients requiring maximum durability on metal, UPVC, GRP, or composite doors. Extremely hardwearing, highly resistant to abrasion and chemical attack, available in all gloss levels

Our Garage Door Spraying Process
1. Free Survey & Colour Consultation
We assess your garage door type, material, and condition — checking for rust, corrosion, peeling coatings, and surface damage. We discuss your chosen colour and finish and confirm the correct coating system for your specific door material.

2. Cleaning & Degreasing
The door is thoroughly pressure washed or hand-cleaned with soap and water to remove all dirt, grime, and loose material. UPVC and powder-coated surfaces are treated with Kolorbond WindowPrep; metal surfaces are degreased with a specialist solvent degreaser.

3. Rust Treatment & Surface Repair
Any surface rust on steel doors is treated with a rust converter or remover to chemically neutralise corrosion. Dents, scratches, holes, and surface damage are filled and feathered before the priming stage.

4. Abrading & Keying
All surfaces — including any raised panel detail, beading, and frame profiles — are lightly sanded or abraded to create the optimal mechanical key for the primer and topcoat.

5. Masking & Protection
The garage floor, brickwork, surrounding fascia, driveway, and all adjacent surfaces are fully protected with static masking film and professional masking tape. The interior of the garage is protected against any overspray.

6. Priming
The appropriate primer system is applied by spray — etch primer for bare metal, Kolorbond-based preparation for UPVC/aluminium — creating a chemically bonded base layer before the colour coat.

7. Topcoat Application
A minimum of two full topcoats are applied using professional HVLP or airless spray equipment, achieving an even, consistent finish across all panels, profiles, and frame elements. Each coat is inspected before the next is applied.

8. De-mask, Inspect & Handover
All masking is removed. We carry out a full quality inspection covering every panel and profile before handover — ensuring every surface meets our exacting standards.
