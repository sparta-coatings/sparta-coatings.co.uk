What Is House Exterior Spraying?
House exterior spraying is a professional airless and HVLP spray application of specialist exterior masonry coatings, elastomeric render paints, and weatherproof systems directly to the outside walls of your property. Rather than applying paint by roller or brush — which leaves lap marks, misses pores, and takes far longer — our airless spray equipment delivers a perfectly even, high-build finish across the entire elevation in a fraction of the time.

It's suitable for virtually all exterior wall types, including:

Smooth and textured sand-and-cement render

Pebbledash and roughcast

K-Rend, monocouche, and thin-coat render

Brick and block

Storm dash and tyrolean finish

Dry dash and exposed aggregate

Mixed substrate properties (brick, render and cladding combined)

The Paints & Products We Use
House exterior spraying requires a completely different set of products from UPVC or metal spraying. The coating system must be breathable, flexible, weatherproof, UV-stable, and specifically formulated for masonry substrates.

Surface Treatments & Primers
Ronseal / Wykamol Biocidal Masonry Wash — Applied to all exterior surfaces first to kill and neutralise all algae, moss, lichen, and fungal growth. Left to dwell and then rinsed — this step is non-negotiable for a lasting finish

Dulux Trade Weathershield Stabilising Primer — Applied to chalky, friable, or powdering masonry surfaces to consolidate and stabilise the substrate before the topcoat. Penetrates deeply and binds loose particles to prevent delamination

Sandtex Trade Alkali-Resistant Primer — Used on new render, fresh cement repairs, or areas of high alkalinity where a direct topcoat application could be compromised by saponification

Zinsser Peel Stop Triple Thick — A high-build, flexible binding primer used on heavily flaking or delaminating exterior surfaces, creating a stable base layer capable of holding a new topcoat system on difficult substrates

Our House Exterior Spraying Process
1. Free Survey & Assessment
We carry out a full site survey — assessing wall type, substrate condition, existing coating adhesion, biological growth levels, cracking, and any areas requiring repair. We confirm the most appropriate coating system and discuss your colour choices.

2. Biocidal Treatment
A professional fungicidal masonry wash is applied across all elevations and left to dwell for the manufacturer's recommended contact time, killing all biological growth. Surfaces are then rinsed clean.

3. Repairs & Preparation
All loose mortar, crumbling render, and delaminating coatings are raked back and removed. Cracks, holes, and damaged render areas are repaired using matching mortar or exterior filler and allowed to fully cure before coating.

4. Priming
The appropriate primer is applied by airless spray — stabilising primer for chalky surfaces, alkali-resistant primer for fresh repairs, or Peel Stop for heavily delaminating areas. This stage is critical for ensuring long-term topcoat adhesion.

5. Masking & Protection
All windows, doors, fascias, soffits, gutters, driveway, paths, and landscaping are fully protected using heavy-duty masking film and professional masking tape. Ground sheets are laid beneath all spray zones to protect hard surfaces from overspray droplets.

6. Topcoat Application — Pass 1
The first full coat of the specified masonry or render topcoat is applied by professional airless spray equipment, achieving full, even penetration across the entire elevation including all recesses, reveals, and surface texture.

7. Topcoat Application — Pass 2 (and Pass 3 where specified)
Once the first coat has dried to the manufacturer's recommended recoat time, a second (and sometimes third) full coat is applied to achieve the specified dry film thickness and full opacity.

8. De-mask, Inspect & Handover
All masking is carefully removed. A full quality inspection is completed — checking coverage, consistency, overspray protection, and edge definition — before handover and sign-off.
