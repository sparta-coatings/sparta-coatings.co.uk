What Is Kitchen Cabinet Spraying?
Kitchen cabinet spraying is a professional on-site process that applies specialist interior wood and furniture coatings directly to your existing kitchen doors, drawer fronts, frames, and end panels — completely transforming their colour and delivering a smooth, durable, factory-quality finish without the cost or disruption of a brand-new kitchen.

Using professional HVLP spray equipment and the correct primer and topcoat system for each specific substrate, our trained applicators achieve results that brushes and rollers simply cannot — a perfectly smooth, streak-free, chip-resistant finish that is built to withstand the heat, moisture, and grease demands of a working kitchen.

Surface Preparation
Tikkurila Cleaning Agent / Professional Kitchen Degreaser — All cabinet surfaces are thoroughly cleaned with a professional degreaser to remove all cooking grease, oils, and surface contamination before any sanding or priming begins. Grease penetrates deeply into kitchen cabinet surfaces and will cause adhesion failure if not fully removed

Helmi Furniture Filler — Tikkurila's own ready-mixed furniture filler, designed specifically for kitchen cabinet preparation. Used to fill all chips, cracks, dowel holes, and surface imperfections before priming. Dries white, is easy to sand, and will not shrink or crack — the manufacturer-recommended filler for use before Helmi topcoats

Mirka Abranet / 180–240 Grit Sanding — All surfaces are hand and machine sanded to create a consistent mechanical key for the primer. MDF edges are sanded and sealed to prevent raised grain before priming

Primers — Correctly Matched by Substrate
Correct primer selection is the most technically important decision in a kitchen respray — the wrong primer on the wrong substrate will fail. We follow the Tikkurila and Morrells manufacturer-specified primer selection exactly:

Tikkurila Helmi Wood Primer (Solid Wood) — The manufacturer-recommended waterborne acrylic primer for use before Helmi topcoat on solid wood and bare wood kitchen cabinets. High-opacity, quick-drying (recoatable in 4 hours), and specifically formulated to seal wood grain and provide the ideal base for the Helmi finish system. Applied by HVLP spray

Tikkurila Otex Akva (Laminate, Veneer & Melamine) — The manufacturer-recommended waterborne adhesion primer for laminate, veneer, melamine-faced, and previously painted kitchen doors where a standard wood primer would not achieve sufficient adhesion. Otex Akva chemically promotes adhesion on difficult, non-porous surfaces — specifically recommended by Tikkurila for these substrates only, not for use as a first primer on bare MDF or solid wood

Morrells 600 / 619 Acid Catalyst White MDF Primer (MDF) — A quick-drying, high-build, acid-catalyst primer specifically formulated for MDF kitchen substrates. Designed for HVLP spray application, seals MDF face and edge porosity, provides excellent sanding properties, and creates the ideal base for the Morrells Acid-Catalyst lacquer topcoat system. Available in white, grey, and tintable black

Tikkurila Intact Primer (Industrially-Painted / Hard Lacquered) — For kitchen doors with an existing industrial paint, hard lacquer, or factory-applied melamine coating that requires specialist adhesion — Intact Primer promotes adhesion on previously coated surfaces including aluminium, zinc, industrial coatings, and hard melamine. Not a substitute for Otex Akva or Helmi Primer on standard surfaces

Zinsser BIN Shellac Primer (Tannin & Stain Blocking) — For solid oak, ash, and other tannin-rich wood kitchens, Zinsser BIN is applied before the main primer to seal and block tannin bleed-through, knots, and any significant staining that would discolour the topcoat

Topcoats
Tikkurila Helmi 30 (Semi-Gloss, 30% Sheen) — The professional standard for kitchen cabinet spraying in the UK. A waterborne interior wood and furniture lacquer engineered specifically for kitchen environments — outstanding scrubbability, moisture resistance, and durability. Helmi 30 is dust-dry in 1 hour, recoatable in 4 hours, and reaches full hardness in 2–3 days. Ultra-low VOC, certified toy-safe, and awarded multiple eco certifications. Available in the full Tikkurila colour range and custom-tinted to match any reference

Tikkurila Helmi 10 (Matte, 10% Sheen) — The matte variant of the Helmi system, delivering the same outstanding durability and kitchen-specific performance in a soft, contemporary finish — increasingly popular on handleless and Shaker-style kitchens

Tikkurila Everal Aqua (Semi-Gloss / Gloss Waterborne) — Tikkurila's waterborne semi-gloss and gloss alternative for kitchen cabinets where a higher sheen level is required. Same low-VOC credentials as Helmi with a harder, more durable, washable film

Morrells Italian Waterborne Lacquer (350 / 450 Series) — A high-performance, water-based pigmented lacquer designed specifically for kitchen cabinet and furniture spraying. The 450 series is catalysed with a hardener to achieve a full 2K-equivalent durability — providing exceptional resistance to moisture, grease, heat, and daily mechanical wear. Available in matte, satin, and semi-gloss

Morrells 600-Series Acid Catalyst Lacquer — The professional workshop-grade acid-catalyst lacquer used in conjunction with Morrells 600/619 MDF primer. This solvent-based, catalysed system cures to an extremely hard, durable film — the preferred choice for kitchens requiring the highest possible resistance to steam, grease, and heat. Applied in two coats over the Morrells MDF primer system

2K Two-Pack Polyurethane Lacquer — For clients requiring maximum durability — particularly in family kitchens, rental properties, or high-traffic environments — a catalysed two-pack PU system is applied over the appropriate primer. This cures chemically to a very hard, chip-resistant, moisture-proof film significantly harder and more durable than any single-pack waterborne system

Our Kitchen Spraying Process
1. Free Survey & Consultation
We visit your home, assess your kitchen's condition, material type, and current finish. We discuss colour options, sheen levels, and recommend the correct primer and topcoat system for your specific cabinet material.

2. Door & Drawer Removal
All cabinet doors and drawer fronts are removed and laid flat for priming and spraying — achieving a far superior finish than coating doors in situ, where runs and sags are far more likely.

3. Surface Preparation & Filling
All surfaces are cleaned with Tikkurila Cleaning Agent and/or professional kitchen degreaser. Chips, cracks, and surface imperfections are filled with Helmi Furniture Filler, allowed to cure, and sanded smooth. All surfaces are then sanded with the appropriate grade to create a consistent mechanical key.

4. Masking & Protection
All worktops, appliances, walls, and flooring are fully protected using masking film and professional tape before spraying begins.

5. Primer Application — Substrate Specific
The correct primer system is applied by HVLP spray — Helmi Wood Primer for solid wood, Otex Akva for laminates and melamine, or Morrells 600/619 for MDF — providing a chemically bonded, smooth base for the topcoat.

6. Inter-Coat Sanding
After the primer coat has fully dried, all surfaces are lightly sanded with fine paper (180–240 grit) to remove any raised grain, dust nibs, or surface imperfections before the topcoat is applied.

7. Topcoat Application — Minimum Two Coats
Two full coats of the specified Helmi, Morrells, or 2K system are applied by HVLP spray with adequate drying time between coats. Each coat is inspected before the next is applied.

8. Reassembly & Final Inspection
Once fully cured, doors and drawers are refitted. We carry out a full quality inspection before leaving, ensuring every surface meets our standards — and yours.

Aftercare & Longevity
Use soft microfibre cloths and mild, non-abrasive detergents for daily cleaning

Wipe up spills immediately — especially oils, acids, and coloured liquids

Avoid scouring pads and harsh chemical cleaners that can dull or damage the coating

Allow full cure time before heavy use — Helmi 30 reaches full hardness in 2–3 days

A properly maintained Helmi or Morrells resprayed kitchen can look pristine for 10 years or more
