What Is Shop Front Spraying?
Shop front spraying is the professional on-site application of specialist coating systems directly to your existing shop front frames, fascia panels, door profiles, transoms, mullions, and associated metalwork — completely changing their colour and refreshing their appearance without the enormous cost of full shop front removal and replacement.

It's the fastest and most cost-effective way to modernise your commercial premises, align your exterior with updated branding, or simply restore a tired, weathered facade to a like-new condition.

Suitable for all commercial shop front types, including:

Aluminium shop fronts (powder-coated, bronze anodised, satin anodised)

UPVC shop front frames and doors

Stainless steel shop fronts and cladding panels

Steel and mild steel shop front sections

Composite and mixed-material facades

Timber shop fronts and fascias

Roller shutters and security grilles

Canopies and entrance canopy frames

Curtain walling sections and glazing bars

The Paints & Products We Use
Shop front spraying is technically demanding — the majority of UK shop fronts are aluminium, either powder-coated or anodised, and both require very specific preparation and primer systems to achieve lasting adhesion. Using the wrong product on an anodised or powder-coated surface will result in delamination. We use only professional, substrate-verified coating systems.

Cleaning & Degreasing
Professional Solvent Degreaser / IPA Wipe-Down — All shop front profiles are thoroughly degreased using isopropyl alcohol or a specialist solvent degreaser to remove all grease, urban grime, atmospheric carbonation, and surface oils before any preparation begins. This is essential on aluminium and anodised surfaces which can accumulate invisible contamination that will prevent adhesion

Mirka Mirlon / 400-grit Wet-and-Dry Paper — All surfaces are mechanically keyed by hand using fine abrading pads — particularly critical on anodised aluminium, which must be scratched through the oxide layer to allow the etch primer to bond to the base metal

Primers
2K Etch Primer (Epoxy Etch Primer) — The foundation of any successful aluminium shop front coating system. A two-component, acid-catalysed etch primer that chemically etches into the aluminium substrate — whether powder-coated, anodised, or bare — creating an exceptionally strong chemical and mechanical bond. This is the non-negotiable primer for all aluminium shop fronts

Rust-Oleum 3302 CombiPrimer Adhesion Primer — A fast-drying, universal adhesion primer for galvanised steel, stainless steel, and powder-coated surfaces where a full 2K etch prime is not required. Provides a chemically bonded base layer for topcoat application on non-ferrous metal shop fronts

Kolorbond Original (UPVC — no primer required) — For UPVC shop front sections, Kolorbond Original is applied directly following thorough preparation with Kolorbond WindowPrep, as it chemically bonds to PVCu without a separate primer step

Zinsser BullsEye 1-2-3 — Applied to timber shop fronts and fascias as a multi-surface stain-blocking primer before the topcoat system

Topcoats
Rust-Oleum Hard Hat (2K Polyurethane Topcoat) — A high-performance two-component aliphatic polyurethane topcoat providing excellent UV stability, gloss retention, hardness, and chemical resistance. Available in gloss, satin, and matte. The professional standard for aluminium, steel, and metal shop front respraying — delivers a true factory-finish appearance that lasts

Kolorbond DecoProt (2K Polyurethane) — For aluminium shop fronts and UPVC sections requiring the highest available durability. Two-pack catalysed PU system in 10%, 30%, 50%, and 70% gloss levels — providing an extremely hard, abrasion, UV, and weather-resistant finish

Sherwin-Williams Hi-Build 2K Polyurethane — For large-scale or high-specification commercial shop front projects requiring a premium, high-gloss, colour-stable topcoat in corporate RAL or BS/NCS/Pantone-referenced shades. Used on major retail and hospitality brand refurbishments across the UK

Kolorbond Original (for UPVC shop fronts) — Single-pack chemically bonding topcoat for PVCu shop front sections, fascia panels, and door frames. 5,000+ colours, 10-year adhesion guarantee

AkzoNobel Interpon D / Dulux Trade Weathershield (for rendered and masonry fascias) — Where the shop front surround includes rendered or masonry panels, we use professional exterior masonry topcoats to ensure a fully cohesive, colour-coordinated finish across the entire facade

Our Shop Front Spraying Process
1. Free Site Survey & Brand Colour Consultation
We assess your shop front material, condition, and extent — advising on the correct coating system, surface preparation requirements, and discussing your brand colour reference, finish level, and any repairs needed. We confirm the most suitable approach for your trading pattern to minimise disruption.

2. Out-of-Hours Scheduling
We work around your business. For retail clients, we typically begin work after closing and aim to have all surfaces dry and back in trading condition before opening the following morning.

3. Masking & Protection
All glazing, signage, and adjacent surfaces are fully masked with professional tape, static masking film, and plastic sheeting to protect every element of your shopfront during spraying.

4. Cleaning & Degreasing
All shop front profiles are thoroughly cleaned and degreased with IPA wipe-down, removing all grease, grime, and atmospheric contamination from the substrate.

5. Mechanical Keying / Abrading
UPVC surfaces are abraded with scotch-brite pads. Aluminium and metal surfaces are keyed with 400-grit wet-and-dry paper, paying particular attention to anodised surfaces which require the oxide layer to be abraded through for etch primer adhesion.

6. In-Situ Repairs
Scratches, chips, dents, and damaged profiles are filled and feathered using appropriate filler systems — aluminium epoxy filler for metal sections, flexible exterior filler for UPVC — achieving a smooth, seamless surface ready for priming.

7. Primer Application
The appropriate 2K etch primer or adhesion primer is applied by HVLP spray, building a fully continuous, chemically bonded base layer across all profiles, frames, and panels.

8. Topcoat Application
A minimum of two full coats of the specified topcoat are applied using professional HVLP spray equipment, achieving an even, consistent finish across every frame, transom, mullion, and fascia panel. Each coat is inspected before the next is applied.

9. De-mask, Inspect & Sign-Off
All masking is carefully removed. A full quality inspection is completed — checking coverage, edge definition, colour consistency, and finish quality — before handover and sign-off.
