What Is Fence & Gate Spraying?
Fence and gate spraying is the professional on-site application of specialist anti-corrosion coating systems directly to your existing metal fencing, railings, gates, and associated ironwork — completely restoring their appearance and protection without removal, without replacement, and without the disruption of having them taken off-site for powder coating.

Using professional HVLP spray equipment and substrate-specific coating systems, our trained applicators deliver an even, consistent finish across even the most complex wrought ironwork, railings, palisade fencing, and decorative metalwork — achieving results that are impossible to replicate with a brush or roller.

The Paints & Products We Use
Metal fencing, gates, and railings present specific coating challenges — wrought iron is highly porous and prone to active rust, galvanised steel requires etching to accept a coating, and powder-coated surfaces need mechanical and chemical keying before a new coat will adhere. We use only professional, substrate-specific products for each type.

Surface Preparation Products
HMG Ferrozinc / Blue Steel Rust Neutraliser — Applied to all areas of active rust on iron and steel fencing and gates. These specialist rust neutralisers chemically convert iron oxide (rust) into a stable, neutral compound — halting corrosion at the surface level before priming and preventing further rust spread beneath the new coating system

Wire Brush / Angle Grinder / Mirka Abranet Discs — All loose and flaking rust, failed paint, and millscale are mechanically removed using wire brushing, angle grinding, and/or Mirka abranet sanding discs before any chemical treatment begins. The goal is to achieve a sound, rust-free substrate

Solvent / IPA Degreaser — All metal surfaces are fully degreased with solvent or isopropyl alcohol wipe-down to remove oils, grease, and contamination before priming

2K Etch Primer Application — Galvanised and aged powder-coated surfaces are etched with a specialist 2K etch primer to break through the oxide or powder-coat layer and create a chemically bonded base for the topcoat

Primers
Manor ZP24 Zinc Phosphate Primer — A professional, xylene-based zinc phosphate primer in red or grey, specifically formulated for ferrous metal fencing, gates, and railings. Zinc phosphate actively inhibits rust by forming a passive layer at the metal surface. Available in red oxide and grey. Can be overcoated with a wide range of single-pack and two-pack topcoats

Avenue Zinc Phosphate Primer — A fast-drying, xylene-based zinc phosphate primer for iron and steel substrates, providing excellent corrosion inhibition before the full topcoat system. Overcoated with Manor Fast Enamel for a traditional high-gloss railing finish

Rust-Oleum CombiColor Anti-Corrosion Primer — A standalone anti-corrosion primer specifically designed for rusted metal, providing certified corrosion inhibition in C3 environments before the full topcoat application. Compatible with CombiColor Original and all Rust-Oleum topcoat systems

Zinsser Cover Stain (Wrought Iron) — For wrought iron gates and railings, Zinsser Cover Stain is applied as a stain-blocking, crack-resistant primer with superior adhesion to iron and steel. Provides excellent enamel undercoating performance and compatibility with all decorative topcoats

2K Epoxy Etch Primer (Galvanised & Powder-Coated) — For galvanised steel palisade fencing and aged powder-coated gates, a two-component acid-etch primer is applied to chemically key the surface before the topcoat — the non-negotiable foundation step for lasting adhesion on these substrates

Our Fence & Gate Spraying Process
1. Free Survey & Condition Assessment
We assess the extent of your fencing and gates, identify the substrate type and condition, check for active rust, failed coatings, damaged sections, and confirm the most appropriate coating specification and colour.

2. Mechanical Preparation
All loose rust, flaking paint, and failed coatings are removed by wire brush, angle grinder, or Mirka abrasive disc — down to a sound, stable substrate.

3. Rust Treatment
Active rust is treated with HMG Ferrozinc or Blue Steel rust neutraliser, chemically converting and stabilising corrosion before any primer is applied.

4. Degreasing
All surfaces are degreased with solvent or IPA wipe-down to remove all oils and contamination.

5. In-Situ Repairs
Damaged sections, broken spindles, and deep pitting are filled and repaired using aluminium epoxy filler before priming.

6. Masking & Protection
Surrounding surfaces — paths, driveways, brickwork, planted areas, and adjacent walls — are fully protected with masking film and ground sheets.

7. Primer Application
The appropriate zinc phosphate, etch, or rust-inhibiting primer is applied by HVLP spray — achieving a fully continuous, corrosion-inhibiting base layer across all bars, spindles, frames, and fixings.

8. Topcoat Application
A minimum of two full coats of the specified topcoat are applied by HVLP spray, achieving even, consistent coverage across the entire fence, gate, and railing system — including all the intricate profiles and joints that a brush cannot reach evenly.

9. De-mask, Inspect & Handover
All masking is removed, gates and mechanisms are checked for free movement, and a full quality inspection is completed before handover.
